InkTank

Context

The tattoo industry generates over 1.65 billion dollars a year and roughly 40% of Americans under the age of 40 have a tattoo. Such a huge pool of potential users is fantastic, but also presents a challenge: how do you create an app that appeals to such a wide variety of people? Additionally, for many users getting a tattoo is a complex, emotional process. How can I utilize this sentiment within an app and avoid creating barriers? In this project, I set out to construct a tattoo-themed app focused on helping users through the process of getting a tattoo, from inspiration to "inked".

Problem Statement

Our users need an easy way to immerse themselves in a community of tattoo artists and enthusiasts; a community that celebrates free-flowing interaction and helps users discover diverse inspiration - because the decision to get a tattoo is sentimental and often complicated. We will know this to be true when we see how many people use our app before and after getting a tattoo.

User Personas

To see the problem statement from a variety of perspectives, and with greater empathy, I created some user personas. Free Spirit (FS) has been steadily adding tattoos for the last eight years of her life and loves the expressive tattoo culture. On the other end of the spectrum, Young Professional (YP) has never gotten a tattoo, feels intimidated by the process, and doesn't consider himself a "tattoo person". Designing an app that meets the needs of these two users, nearly polar opposites in the context of tattoos and tattoo culture, would produce an effective, enjoyable app with widespread appeal.




User Flows

The next step toward an MVP was imagining how my user personas would navigate some of the app's primary features. Being anxious about this tattoo being perfect, YP would use the AR tool often to test his choices and reassure himself. FS would spend a lot of time on the artist bios, digging into the creative process and interacting with artists.
